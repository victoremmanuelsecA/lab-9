Animal Museum

Mammals

Mammals are warm-blooded animals with hair or fur. They give birth to live young and feed them with milk produced by mammary glands.

Fish

Fish are cold-blooded animals that live in water. They have gills that allow them to breathe underwater and fins that help them swim.

Birds

Birds are warm-blooded animals with feathers, wings, and beaks. Most birds can fly, and they lay eggs with hard shells.

Reptiles

Reptiles are cold-blooded animals that have scales and lay eggs. They include snakes, lizards, turtles, and crocodiles.

Amphibians

Amphibians are cold-blooded animals that spend part of their life in water and part on land. They include frogs, toads, and salamanders.
